AI surveillance is what Monitor is all about we want you to know just how monitored you are, but do you know just how much?

To tell you just how monitored you are we took help from Steve Nouri who is the Head of Data Science and AI at Australian Computer Society. According to Steve there is millions of cameras operating 24/7 all over the globe but noone is operating them as the AI does all the work for the man. Humans would not be able to work 24/7 but AI however can process every frame and present real-time analysis to save millions of lives. These AI surveillance technologies can map, track and control people to meet various policy aims and even some legitimate, some violating human rights and some which fall into a gray area.

AI is the future for many kinds of work. To quote Steve :
“The reason AI is making a vast impact is because of its self-learning capabilities. Initially, the computer learned to reason about objects of interest in the scene. Now, it detects the objects in the real world, matches the results with the correct annotations (provided by humans) and tries to improve further. This way, it is constantly learning and improving. Error is minimized with every iteration and moves closer to — or even better than — human error.”
